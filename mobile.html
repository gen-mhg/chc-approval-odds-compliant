Need Help?

Will checking my free credit report via MoneyHero negatively affect my credit score?▼
No, checking your own credit report for personal review will not negatively impact your credit score. Only "hard inquiries," which occur when you apply for new credit, can have a minor, temporary effect.
How often should I check my credit report?▼
We recommend checking at least once every 3 months. Look for unauthorized accounts, incorrect information, and changes to your credit utilization.
How does Credit Hero protect my data?▼
Your data is encrypted and securely transmitted. Raw credit data is purged within 24 hours. MoneyHero does not share your credit data with lenders or third parties.
What should I do if I find incorrect information?▼
Contact TransUnion directly to dispute any inaccuracies. You can also reach out to our support team for guidance.

🔒 See Your Approval Odds

We can show you how likely you are to be approved for each product based on your TransUnion credit data.

  • See High / Medium / Low approval odds for every product
  • All products remain visible — nothing is hidden or filtered
  • Products will be sorted by your approval odds (highest first)
  • Odds are informational only and do not guarantee approval
  • Your credit data is not shared with lenders or third parties
How it works: Your existing TransUnion credit profile (already retrieved for your credit report) will be compared against general product requirements to generate approval likelihood indicators. No additional credit data is accessed.

What are Approval Odds?

Approval odds indicate your general likelihood of being approved for a product, based on your TransUnion credit profile compared against historical application data.

高 High
High likelihood. Your credit profile meets most or all typical requirements.
中 Med
Moderate likelihood. Your credit profile meets some but not all typical requirements.
低 Low
Lower likelihood. Your credit profile may not meet typical requirements.
Important: Approval odds are informational only and do not guarantee approval or rejection. The final decision always rests with the lender.
