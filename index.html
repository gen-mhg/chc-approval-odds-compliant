Tips to Improve Your Credit Score

Always pay your bills on time▼
Payment history is one of the biggest factors in your credit score. Late payments can stay on your record for years. Set up automatic payments or calendar reminders to ensure you never miss a due date.
Keep your credit card balances low▼
Try to keep your credit utilisation ratio below 30%. Lower utilisation shows lenders you're managing credit responsibly.
Avoid applying for too many credit products at once▼
Each credit application generates a hard inquiry on your report. Multiple inquiries in a short period can lower your score.
Check your credit report regularly▼
Regular monitoring helps you spot errors or fraudulent activity early. Dispute any inaccuracies promptly.

Your Approval Odds

What do the levels mean?

高
High Approval Odds
Based on users with a similar credit profile, there's a strong chance you'll be approved for this product. The terms shown (interest rate, fees) are indicative and may change after final lender assessment.
中
Medium Approval Odds
There's a reasonable chance of approval, but it's not certain. Other factors such as income, employment status, and existing debt may affect the lender's final decision.
低
Low Approval Odds
Based on similar credit profiles, approval is less likely. You may still apply, but consider that an unsuccessful application will add a hard enquiry to your credit report.

Approval odds do not guarantee approval

Your approval odds are an estimate based on data from users with similar credit profiles. They are provided for informational purposes only and do not constitute a guarantee of approval or rejection by any lender. Each lender has their own assessment criteria that may include factors beyond your credit report.

How are approval odds calculated?

Your approval odds are derived from your credit profile provided by TransUnion, including your credit score, credit grade, and credit history. We compare your profile against historical approval data from users with similar credit characteristics to estimate the likelihood of approval for each product.

💡
How approval odds help you

Every time you apply for a credit product, the lender performs a "hard search" on your credit report. Multiple hard searches in a short period can lower your credit score and signal financial stress to future lenders.

By focusing on products where you have higher approval odds, you can reduce the number of unsuccessful applications — and avoid unnecessary hard searches that could hurt your credit score.

Things to keep in mind

• Approval odds are updated when you refresh your credit report
• Your odds may change as your credit profile changes
• Lenders may consider information not available in your credit report (e.g. income verification, employment)
• Always review the full product terms before applying
